The worst web site in Ireland must surely be that of our Government's Department of Education and Science (worst, given its avowed aim to have all citizens be web literate etc, and given its resources...). Its Contact Addresses and Numbers corner is - in my view - utterly useless, since it names not a single official, nor gives any email addresses... Who, for example, is the Chief Inspector for Mathematics? One might wish to write to him/her...

(Once I did want to write to the Chief Inspector for Mathematics, and was reduced to phoning the department to find out his/her name. I was given a name, and wrote to him about an important matter. Five weeks passed without an acknowledgement (one is not surprised by that, but at the same time...), and I foolishly wrote again: when might I expect to hear ... ? The recipient wrote to tell me that he was not the Chief Inspector for Mathematics; rather it was someone else. The someone else had been in receipt of my letter... but just hadn't bothered to reply to me...)

Even if one goes to The Management of the Department section, one finds not a single contact detail (phone, fax, email).

I have been writing to persons in that department (including its Secretary-General) on and off about this matter since April 1996 (when it launched its web site), but no one there appears to give a toss. When its site was first launched the then minister invited the site's "customers" (ugh!) to comment on it, and I wrote that unless it hugely improved the content, it would soon cease to have any customers. By way of a standard for it to aim for I suggested it seek out the (then) worst known site in the world and aim that high; then it might be getting somewhere. Now, over four years later, it hasn't even reached that high.
