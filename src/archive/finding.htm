The greatest resource on the Web for prime numbers is Chris Caldwell's remarkable site The Prime Pages.

On 6th September 1996, Slowinski and Gage using a Cray supercomputer announced the discovery of a new record prime number, the Mersenne prime 21257787 - 1.
     Within months - on 13th November 1996 - that record had been broken by Armengaud and Woltman, the first triumph of George Woltman's inspired Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search (GIMPS) project. They announced the discovery of the prime 21398269 - 1.
     In January 1997 I signed up myself and my two colleagues, Olivia Bree and Mark Daly, with the GIMPS project.
