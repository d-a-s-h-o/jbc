Conway's Look-and-Say algebraic number

Perhaps the most remarkable example of an algebraic number (from a gee-whiz point of view) is provided by John Conway's Look -and- Say sequence (see the wonderful Book of Numbers by Conway & Guy):

1, 11, 21, 1211, 111221, 312211, 13112221, 1113213211, 31131211121221, ...

I hope you see why it's called the Look-and-Say sequence... (If you don't already know of N. J. A. Sloane's extraordinary On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences, then you simply must consult it; be prepared, however, to suffer the disappointment of thinking you've discovered a new ( interesting ) sequence: you will almost certainly find it there...).
