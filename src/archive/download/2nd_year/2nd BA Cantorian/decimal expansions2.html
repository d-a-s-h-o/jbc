Decimal (i.e. base 'base' 10) expansions of rational numbers . Every rational number (at a sophisticated level those may be viewed as being algebraic numbers of degree 1 ) has a decimal expansion which is one of three types:

  • terminating (examples (i), (ii), (iii) and (iv) above)
  • pure periodic after the decimal point (examples (v) and (viii) above)
  • eventually periodic (examples (vi) and (vii) above)
