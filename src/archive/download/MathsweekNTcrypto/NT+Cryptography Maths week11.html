Three book recommendations

1. When I was still at school I had the great good fortune to come upon a book, FROM ZERO TO INFINITY , by Constance Reid; that book determined my mathematical life. Although not herself a mathematician, Constance Reid's sister Julia Robinson was a leading mathematician (she was the first woman to become President of the (prestigious) American Mathematical Society), and Constance Reid learned a great deal of number theory from her sister Julia, and also from Julia's husband, Raphael Robinson (a leading number theorist).

A fiftieth anniversary edition of Constance Reid's book was published earlier this year, and I quote the view of one renowned mathematician/physicist:

" From Zero To Infinity is a sequence of rare beauty,
a drama built out of nothing but numbers - and imagination."
FREEMAN DYSON (Princeton Institute for Advanced Studies)

2. In Code ( A Mathematical Journey ) by remarkable young Irish woman Sarah Flannery, and her co-author father David Flannery (mathematician, and teacher extraordinaire). Sarah's book gives the background to her winning - at the age of 16 - of the 1999 Irish Young Scientist of the Year award for her creation of what Sarah called the Cayley-Purser (cryptographic) algorithm .

3. The outstanding general history of cryptography reference is David Kahn's 1181 page The Codebreakers (The Comprehensive History of Secret Communication from Ancient Times to the Internet), published by SCRIBNER , 1996 (2nd. new edition) ISBN 0-684-83130-9. (Incidentally, there is an excellent journal, Cryptologia - produced by the United States Military Academy at Fort Worth - which treats the history of cryptography.)
