Title of talk

Number Theory and Cryptography
 (A Maple-based introduction)

For Wilfrid Keller

A Mathematics Week (Ireland) talk for secondary school students
Thursday, 19 October 2006

John B. Cosgrave, Mathematics Department,
St. Patricks College, Drumcondra, Dublin 9, Ireland

email: John.Cosgrave@spd.dcu.ie

website: www.spd.dcu.ie/johnbcos

This talk is a much altered version of my Bill Clinton, Bertie Ahern, and digital signatures lecture delivered at the Sixteenth International Conference on Technology in Collegiate Mathematics (ICTCM16), in Chicago, Illinois, October 30 - November 2, 2003. Anyone interested in obtaining my Chicago lecture - or this talk - may do so in the Public and Other Lectures corner of my web site. I have converted all such talks to html format so they may be read by anyone with internet access. I also make available at my web site the original Maple files, and thus anyone who has Maple may introduce their own variations into my worksheet (providing they know how to do so).

Here in St Patrick's College, since 1995-96, I have taught a 3rd year undergraduate course Number Theory and Cryptography

( using Maple ). A fairly full outline of my course is available in:

Number Theory and Cryptography ( using Maple ) in David Joyner USNA (Ed.),
Coding Theory and Cryptography: From Enigma to Geheimschreiber to Quantum Theory
(Unites States Naval Academy Conference), Springer-Verlag, 2000, pp 124-143.

All of those papers, including ones by two renowned English mathematicians Peter Hilton and William Tutte (1917-2002) - both of whom worked in the famous Bletchley Park decryption centre (Enigma machine etc) during the second world war - are available in pdf format at the United States Naval Academy site http://web.usna.navy.mil/~wdj/papers/cryptoday.html

(Informative obituary notices (in the Times , Guardian , Independent , etc) for William Tutte are available at the extraordinary St Andrews University History of Mathematics site at http://www-gap.dcs.st-and.ac.uk/~history/Mathematicians/Tutte.html )
